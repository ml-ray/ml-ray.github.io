ML‑RAY

Automated Vulnerability Assessment of Public Machine Learning Models

Abstract

Machine learning models have grown exponentially in recent years, supporting various real‑world applications. Popular ML platforms such as Hugging Face host thousands of publicly shared models, but with convenience comes risk: the prevalence of hidden vulnerabilities. In this paper, we introduce ML‑RAY, the first automated framework for security testing of public ML models at scale. Evaluating 1,381 popular models — serving 19 million+ users — under eight representative adversarial attacks, we reveal that 70% of models are vulnerable, losing 50%+ accuracy under black‑box attacks. Lightweight edge‑deployed models fare worst: 90% attack success. Our findings urge platform‑integrated, automated vulnerability assessment.
